Device Incompatibility Caused by User-Defined FusionDirector Certificate

Symptom

The user-defined FusionDirector certificate of the Atlas 500 A2 edge station may not meet the requirements of the Atlas 500 A2 edge station. In this case, you need to replace the certificate with a new one, but the new certificate will make the Atlas 500 A2 edge station fail to be managed.

Possible Cause

A user wants to add the Atlas 500 A2 edge station when the Atlas 500 A2 edge station is used. However, the Atlas 500 A2 edge station has stricter requirements on the FusionDirector certificate, such as the need for a SAN and an RSA key length of 3072. As a result, the original user-defined certificate may not meet the requirements and needs to be replaced with a new one.

If SAN is contained in the certificated exported from FusionDirector, the preceding problem does not occur.

Solution

  1. Create a new certificate that meets the requirements of the Atlas 500 A2 edge station according to its certificate format.
  2. On the FusionDirector WebUI, import the new certificate to all managed Atlas 500 A2 edge stations.
  3. Update the FusionDirector certificate and make it take effect. Wait until the Atlas 500 A2 edge station is automatically managed.
  4. After the certificate takes effect, manage the Atlas 500 A2 edge station using the new certificate.

    Check whether the fault is rectified.
